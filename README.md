# Ultimate 3s Up
A variation of the classic low-wins dice game, 3s up.


# Pieces
52 dice

25 Minor dice: d6

25 Major dice: d3, d4, d5, d6, d7, d8, d9, d10, d11, d12, d13, d14, d15, d16, d17, d18, d19, d20, d21, d22, d24, d30, d36, d48, d60

2 Greater dice: d100, d120


# Scoring
This is a low score game; Add the values of all dice you save; Lowest score wins.

Any result of 3 counts as 0.

On the d10, the 3 face and the 0 face both count as 0.

On the d36, the 3 face and the 33 face both count as 0.

All other results count as face value.


# Setup
Every player antes into the pot.

Each player rolls two d6 to determine first player. 3s count as 0 for this roll.

The lowest total goes first. If the lowest result is tied, only the tied lowest players roll again until one player is lowest. 

Turn order then proceeds to the left of the winner.

The winner of the pot goes first next round. If the pot is split, the tied players each roll two d6 again using the same starting rule; The lowest total goes first next round.


# Round Sequence
Each active player takes one full turn using the full 52-dice set.

After all active players finish, the lowest score wins the pot.

Ties for lowest score split the pot evenly.


# Turn Sequence
At the start of your turn, roll all 52 dice.

After each roll, choose dice to save and set aside. Saved dice are not rolled again.

Reroll every die you did not save.

Continue until all dice have been saved. Your saved dice total is your score.


# Climbing Keep Rule
The minimum number of dice you must save rises with the roll number.

Roll 1: Save 0 or more dice.

Roll 2: Save at least 1 die.

Roll 3: Save at least 2 dice.

Roll 4: Save at least 3 dice.

Continue the same pattern for later rolls.


# Betting
Only the first roll of each player's turn creates a betting round.

After a player makes that first roll and chooses which dice to keep, a betting round begins before that player makes a second roll.

Starting with that player and continuing around the table, each player still in the round may call the current amount, raise, go all in, or fold.

If a player raises, every remaining player has another chance to call, raise, go all in or fold. Betting continues until all remaining players have settled the final amount.

If a raise is more than a player has left, that player may go all in with everything they have remaining.

Any amount bet above that all-in amount goes into a side pot the all-in player cannot win.

If the all-in player wins the main pot, the side pot is split evenly among the players who contributed to it and did not fold. If an eligible player wins the round, that player wins both the main pot and the side pot.

After the betting round ends, the current player completes the rest of their turn with no more betting. Each players first roll and first keep selection begins a new round of betting.


# Strategy Guide
1. Protect cheap dice for later. Don't burn all your easy dice early. The minor dice and other small dice are future padding when the keep requirement gets larger.

2. Capture bargain rolls on big dice. Many of the biggest swings come from the larger major dice. Especially the greater dice, the King (d120) and the Queen (d100). If one of those comes up very low early, it is often worth saving immedietly.

3. Opening Rolls: harvest, do not gorge. Early on, favor zeros, excellent medium rolls, and clearly underpriced large-die results. Do not automatically save every low small die you see.

4. Middle and late rolls: loosen gradually. As the keep requirement climbs, your standards should loosen. Late in the turn, damage control matters more than perfection.

5. Play to the target. If the best posted score is mediocre, play balanced and avoid a blowup. If the best posted score is very low, get stingier early and chase a lower ceiling. A score above 150 isn't great, a score around 115 - 130 is decent, and a score below 100 is very good.

6. Betting rules of thumb: raise mainly when your opener is genuinely strong, especially if a giant die rolled very low. Late position is stronger for betting because you already know the scores to beat. small raises usually build the pot; large raises create real fold pressure.



# Quick Reference
Win condition: lowest total score wins the pot.

Betting Round: after each player's first roll and first keep selection.

Keep ladder: by roll number, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5...

Pot Format: main pot plus side pots when a player goes all in.
